## Execution Power Hits Zero: The Endgame of Old Professions Seen Through the Disappearance of Coachmen

The historical patterns of the Industrial Revolution are replaying in the AI era, but this time it's far more thorough. In the past, we always fantasized that old professions would transform, much like assuming coachmen could become train drivers. But the fact is, coachmen simply disappeared. In today's practical combat, I've seen the model where traditional designers earn money through "drawing execution" completely collapse. When AI can finish in minutes what used to take hours, the industry itself ceases to exist. My core cognition has undergone a fundamental shift: AI assistance isn't about saving old jobs; it's declaring the end of old professions and the birth of new identities. In this new world, the only rule for survival is no longer hand speed or software proficiency, but whether you own a private Agent that encapsulates your unique aesthetics and decision logic.

## Chatting Is Development: Solidifying Human Judgment into the Prototype of an Agent

If execution power is no longer valuable, where does human value lie? Today's cover design实战 (practical combat) gave me the answer. While having Lobster generate covers, I didn't just accept the first result. Instead, I went through a series of critical "judgment moments": I rejected the proposal where the "Cyber fusion was too flashy," pointed out that "the lightbulb was redundant," established the aesthetic standard that "real people appearing is better than faceless figures," made directional choices in the v2 version, and finally summarized the rule that "expressions must be either authentic or exaggerated." These moments—aesthetics, direction, rejection, correction, and pattern judgment—are the irreplaceable core of humans. So-called "chatting is development" is essentially feeding these continuous judgments into the AI and solidifying them into templates. The `youtube-thumbnail-system.md` file I now possess is no longer just a document; it is the prototype of my "Design Agent." It records all my decision logic, ensuring the next collaboration doesn't start from scratch.

## Today's Takeaways

- **Stop Guessing Job Titles**: Don't waste time defining what an "AI-era designer" is called. Start building the Agent system that carries your judgment immediately.
- **Identify the Five Judgment Moments**: Deliberately capture aesthetics, direction, rejection, correction, and pattern judgment during collaboration. These are the core materials for solidifying into an Agent.
- **Zero Execution Power Is the Norm**: Accept the fact that old professions vanish because their execution capabilities are replaced. Shift your energy from "how to do it" to "what to do" and "why do it."
- **Documentation Is the Agent Prototype**: Record every high-quality decision process as a structured template (such as `youtube-thumbnail-system.md`). This is the early form of a private Agent.
- **Judgment Is the Only Moat**: If you do only one thing, make it cultivating and solidifying your judgment. This is the sole source of professional identity in the new era.
